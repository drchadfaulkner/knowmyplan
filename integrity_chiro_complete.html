Report of Findings — Patient Education

This form explains the three phases of healing to the patient. Review each phase with them before presenting the care plan and financial options.
PHASE 1 — INFLAMMATORY PHASE (0–72 hours)
This phase begins from the moment of injury up to 72 hours after impact. During this phase the injured person may not feel pain, however tissues within the bony structures and nerves will begin a process of ramping up inflammation to begin producing scar tissue. Think of the injury like someone has punched a hole through drywall — the scar tissue is the putty and tape used to repair it.
  • Chiropractic Adjustments
  • Passive and Active Range of Motion
  • Electric Muscle Stimulation
  • Traction Therapy
  • Home Exercise Bands and/or Medical Equipment
PHASE 2 — REPAIR PHASE (up to 6 weeks)
The body makes all scar tissue repairs needed up to 6 weeks after the injury. This is the time the body should be getting chiropractic and physical medicine care to make sure alignment of muscles, bones, and ligaments are properly restored. Think of this phase as concrete that is still setting — there is time to mold the areas and heal the structures. Important: scar tissue manufactured to heal is only approximately 70% as strong as original tissue. Scar tissue is laid down in a haphazard way, creating trigger points and knots that bring extra pain fibers. Bio-electricity disruption can cause pain to refer to other sites.
PHASE 3 — REMODELING / CORRECTIVE PHASE
The remodeling phase focuses on restoring proper movement patterns, strengthening stabilizing muscles, and correcting structural alignment. Continued chiropractic care during this phase helps ensure that scar tissue remodels in an organized, functional pattern. Home exercise compliance is critical to long-term outcomes.
